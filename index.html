About Me

I am a Machine Learning/Optical Engineer, currently fulfilling my national defense duty in South Korea. My work and research combine various fields from digital holography and photonics to on-device AI and computer vision.

I graduated from the Department of Electronic Engineering at Kyungpook National University in March 2024. I maintained a GPA of 4.12/4.5 and minored in Computer Engineering. During my undergraduate years, I served as the leader of BITDOL (Hardware Research Group), where I secured funding of 55M KRW(~40,000 USD) and oversaw various projects. Through these experiences, I developed extensive expertise in designing and prototyping diverse embedded systems.

My journey in the field of optics began as an undergraduate researcher in a Vision & Imaging Lab, where I was fascinated by the potential of enhancing and synthesizing images. This interest evolved into a passion for combining deep learning with traditional image processing to solve real-world challenges.

Currently, my research focuses on the intersection of computational imaging, computer vision, and embedded systems. I'm particularly interested in developing novel algorithms that can operate efficiently on-device for applications in autonomous systems, surveillance, and mobile photography.

My hobbies include drawing, media art, collecting vinyl LPs, and visiting exhibitions. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to chat over coffee!
